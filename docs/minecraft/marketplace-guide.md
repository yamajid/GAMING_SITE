# Minecraft Marketplace Guide 2026

The Minecraft Marketplace is Bedrock Edition's official content store — skins, texture packs, maps, and experiences created by official partners. Here's how to navigate it, what's worth buying, and how to get as much as possible for free.

## What the Marketplace Sells

The Marketplace has four main content categories:

**Skin packs** — Collections of 10–40 character skins for your avatar. Range from 160–1,340 Minecoins. Individual skins in the Character Creator are 100–500 Minecoins each.

**Texture packs** — Overhauls the visual style of the entire game. Range from 660–1,340 Minecoins. High-quality packs from studios like Razzleberries or Noxcrew are popular.

**Maps and worlds** — Pre-built worlds including adventure maps, mini-games, survival starts, and story experiences. Range from 660–1,340 Minecoins.

**Mash-up packs** — Bundles that combine skins, textures, and worlds in a single themed package (Minecraft x Star Wars, Minecraft x Halo, etc.). Typically 1,340 Minecoins.

## What's Available for Free

Before spending a single Minecoin, check what's available at no cost:

**Free Marketplace filter** — In the Marketplace, filter by "Free" to see current free content. Mojang regularly adds free maps, mini-games, and experiences. As of 2026, dozens of free items are available including full adventure maps and seasonal content.

**Character Creator free items** — The Character Creator dressing room has hundreds of free cosmetic items (accessories, clothing, expressions) that don't require Minecoins. Check it monthly for new additions.

**Seasonal free content** — During holidays and Minecraft anniversaries, Mojang adds temporary free Marketplace content. Watch the official Minecraft social accounts for announcements.

**Java Edition skins (free)** — If you play Java Edition, the skin system is completely free — upload any 64×64 PNG image as your skin. Thousands of community-made skins are available free on sites like NameMC, The Skindex, and Planet Minecraft.

## Best Value Paid Content

If you're spending Minecoins, here's what offers the best value:

**Mash-up packs** — At 1,340 Minecoins, these bundle skins + textures + world together. Buying these components separately would cost 3,000+ Minecoins, making mash-ups roughly 2× better value.

**Large skin packs (660–1,340 coins)** — Packs with 30–40 skins offer 25–50 Minecoins per skin, while individual Character Creator items can cost 100–500 Minecoins each.

**Event-tied content** — Minecraft occasionally offers limited-time content at a discount during events. These deals don't last long but offer the best prices on premium content.

## Content to Avoid

**Mini skin packs (160 Minecoins for 6 skins)** — Poor value compared to larger packs. The per-skin cost is higher than buying a full 40-skin pack.

**Individual Character Creator items at 500 Minecoins** — Some items are overpriced relative to what full skin packs offer. Compare per-skin costs before buying.

**Anything you can get free** — Always check the free section and seasonal promotions before paying for content.

## Marketplace vs Community Content

A common question: why pay for Marketplace content when free community content exists?

**Marketplace advantages:**
- Officially tested and safe — no malware risk
- Works on all platforms including console and mobile
- Automatic updates
- Server-compatible for multiplayer experiences

**Community content advantages:**
- Usually free
- Enormous variety (thousands of options)
- Often higher technical quality for experienced players

For mobile and console players, the Marketplace is often the only practical option. For PC Java Edition players, community content is usually the better choice.
