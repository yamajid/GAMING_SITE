# Fortnite Promo Codes — March 2026

**Last verified:** March 15, 2026 ✅

:::info How V-Bucks codes work
Official V-Bucks codes are purchased cards (sold at GameStop, Amazon, etc.) — Epic does not release "free V-Bucks promo codes." Any site claiming otherwise is a scam.

## What Fortnite Does Offer for Free

Epic Games does not give away V-Bucks via promo codes the way Roblox does. However, you can legitimately earn V-Bucks through:

- **Save the World daily quests** — 50–150 V-Bucks/day for active players
- **Battle Pass challenges** — Earn V-Bucks back as you level up the pass
- **Battle Pass level-up rewards** — The pass pays for itself if you reach level ~150
- **Epic promotions** — Occasional bonus events (check Epic's official site)

See our full [Free V-Bucks guide](/docs/fortnite/free-vbucks-guide) for details on all methods.

## Creator Support-A-Creator Codes

Using a creator code doesn't give YOU V-Bucks — it gives the creator a small commission from purchases you make. But if you're buying V-Bucks anyway, supporting a creator costs you nothing extra.

Popular creator codes: `NINJA`, `TFUE`, `LOEYA`, `FRESH`

Enter them in the Fortnite item shop under "Support a Creator."

## How to Spot Fortnite V-Bucks Scams

Every "free V-Bucks code generator" online is a scam. Here's how they work:

1. They promise a code via a website
2. They make you complete surveys or share your account
3. They either steal your Epic account or just never deliver
4. Epic Games has **no API** that allows third parties to generate V-Bucks

**Real rule:** If a site or person is offering free V-Bucks codes that weren't purchased at retail, it's a scam. No exceptions.
